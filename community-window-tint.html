Georgia Window Tint Law: What Every Driver Needs to Know

If you're thinking about tinting your car windows in Georgia, it's essential to understand what the law allows — and what it doesn't. I've seen first-hand how small mistakes can lead to costly tickets or even a court appearance. That's why I want to walk you through the Georgia window tint law step by step, so you can stay compliant and avoid unnecessary headaches.

What the Georgia Window Tint Law Says

Under Georgia window tint law, your car windows must allow more than 32% light transmission. In other words, your tint must be at least 32% transparent. The lower the number, the darker the tint. For example, 5% "limo tint" is far too dark and is illegal in this state.

Contrary to what many people think, you can't tint your back windows darker than your front ones. The 32% transparency rule applies to all windows — front, sides, and rear.

To make sure your tint is legal, I recommend having it measured with a tint meter. I carry one myself. This device clamps onto the window and gives an accurate reading. Georgia allows a margin of error of about 3%, so if your reading is 29% or higher, you're still in the clear. Anything below that crosses the legal line.

What if You're from Out of State?

I often get asked: what happens if you're just driving through Georgia?

Let's say you're from Florida, where window tint as dark as 20% is legal. You have Florida plates, and you're stopped in Georgia because of your tint. In most cases, law enforcement will respect your home state's laws — as long as your license plate clearly shows your out-of-state registration. So if your tint is legal in Florida, you're not breaking the law just by passing through Georgia.

However, be careful if you've recently moved. If you now live in Georgia but kept your darker tint, you could run into trouble once you switch to Georgia plates.

Don't Let a Shop Mislead You

Unfortunately, not every tint shop understands or follows the Georgia window tint law. I've seen cases where a shop installed tint that was far too dark, and the customer had to peel it off, spend more money to re-tint the windows, and appear in court just to show they fixed the issue.

Before you let anyone touch your car windows, make sure they know the current law. Ask them directly: "Will this tint meet the Georgia window tint law?" Don't assume they'll get it right.

My First Trial Was About Window Tint

Believe it or not, my very first trial as an attorney involved a window tint ticket.

Here's what happened: a police officer measured the tint on the front window, then assumed the same tint had been applied to the back and rear windows — without actually measuring those. He issued multiple citations based on that assumption.

That's not how the law works.

In court, I pointed out that the officer wasn't a certified expert and didn't follow proper procedure. The judge agreed and tossed out the charge for the rear window. That experience taught me an important lesson: never accept assumptions when the law demands evidence and precision.

Exceptions for Medical Conditions

There is one legal exception to the Georgia window tint law: medical necessity.

If you have a condition like photophobia (light sensitivity), you may qualify for darker tint. Talk to your optometrist. If they agree, they can complete a form provided by the Georgia Department of Driver Services (DDS). Once approved, you're allowed to go darker than 32%.

Keep in mind that even with medical approval, limo tint (5%) is still not permitted. But you'll have more flexibility than the average driver.

Georgia Window Tint Law: Additional FAQs

Can I get pulled over just for window tint in Georgia?

Yes. Window tint violations are a primary offense in Georgia. That means an officer can legally stop you solely for suspected illegal tint — even if you're not breaking any other laws.

Does Georgia require a window tint sticker?

Yes. Georgia law requires a certification sticker placed between the tint and glass on each tinted window. This sticker shows the film meets state guidelines. A missing sticker may raise suspicion and lead to a stop — even if your tint percentage is legal.

Do SUVs and trucks follow the same window tint rules in Georgia?

Yes. While federal guidelines allow darker tint on rear windows of some trucks or vans, Georgia state law does not distinguish between vehicle types. All windows on all vehicles must meet the 32% minimum transparency requirement.

How can I legally darken my windows more in Georgia?

You need a medical exemption. Here's the process:

  1. Consult an optometrist or physician about your condition.
  2. Complete the Georgia DDS medical waiver form with their approval.
  3. Keep the approved documentation in your vehicle at all times.

Final Thoughts on Staying Legal

If you're planning to tint your windows in Georgia, take a few minutes to verify that you're staying within the law. Don't rely on guesses. Know your percentages, use a tint meter if needed, and double-check the shop's knowledge of Georgia window tint law.

Getting it right the first time will save you money, stress, and legal trouble down the road. And if you ever find yourself dealing with a ticket you believe was issued unfairly, know that you have rights — and a legal process that must be followed.
